Brendan Fraser

This is Brendan Fraser on the set of Scrubs.

Brendan Fraser is a seasoned Canadian-American actor known for many roles.

He has been nominated for and won many major awards, most recently winning the Screen Actors Guild and Academy Awards for the 2022 film The Whale. He rose to prominence in the 90's after portraying a revived caveman in Encino Man. In the mid-90's, he also had starring roles in Airheads and School Ties. The latter were not incredible box office successes, but this did not stop Fraser.

After the successes of George of the Jungle and The Mummy in the late 90's, Fraser was established as a star. He went on to give many more memorable performances in the coming years, such as in Blast from the Past and Bedazzled.

In the early 2000's, Fraser starred on stage in Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, as well as making many more film and TV appearances. His stint on Scrubs is legendary with fans to this day. Inkheart and Journey to the Center of the Earth were two novels adapted for film featuring Fraser.

Fraser's career fluctuated for about a decade between 2010-2020. Most of the films he was in were not very successful, and then in 2018 Fraser came out with allegations against a Hollywood bigwig. Hollywood treated him terribly for being vocal about his experiences as a survivor of abuse. Thankfully, Fraser did not let this stop him.

When the 2020s rolled around, so did what his fans have dubbed "The Brennaisance". Fraser made a comeback in films such as No Sudden Move and Darren Aronofsky's The Whale. His performance in The Whale was critically acclaimed and he recieved a six-minute standing ovation at the premiere at the Venice International Film Festival. This is typically noted as the turning point in his modern career. He also had a role in the hugely popular Killers of the Flower Moon, and even though it was not a huge part, he stood out to critics and viewers alike.

As for the future, it looks so bright he has to wear shades. Fans both old and new alike are looking forward to Fraser's next performances. Brothers, directed by Max Barbakow, is set to release this year. Rental Family, directed by Hikari, is in post-production, and Pressure, where he will be portraying Dwight D. Eisenhower, is in pre-production.
